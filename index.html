Mass: kg

Linear Velocity: m/s

Equation: AngularVelocity = -mtvtrt / ½mdrd2+mtrt2

Calculated Disk Angular Velocity : rad/s

Derivation of Equation

Li = Lf
0 = Lt+Ld
Lt = -Ld
It⍵t/g = -Id⍵d/g
It(⍵t/d+⍵d/g) = -Id⍵d/g
Id⍵d/g+It⍵d/g = -It⍵t/d
⍵d/g = -It⍵t/d / Id+It
⍵d/g = -mtrt2vt/rt / ½mdrd2+mtrt2
⍵d/g = -mtvtrt / ½mdrd2+mtrt2

Notes:

-vt means linear speed of the train with respect to the disk
-If this problem had no relative speed involved, we could just say:
⍵d = -mtvtrt / Id
But since it's realtive speed, we can't exactly do that. Conceptually, that's why our final equation is that but with the moment of inertia of the train also in the denominator
